A lesson creator wants to build a new AutismGPT lesson for a child with autism.

TOPIC / OBJECTIVE: "{idea}"

Your task has three parts:
1. IDENTIFY the skill type this topic belongs to.
2. BRAINSTORM the best teaching approach for this specific topic with a child with autism.
3. WRITE a short Nessa-child session script demonstrating that approach.

━━━ CRITICAL RULES ━━━
- Read the topic carefully. Choose a teaching approach that naturally fits IT — not one that fits past lesson examples.
- Do NOT default to vocabulary / word definition format unless the topic explicitly says "learn the meaning of words" or "vocabulary."
- Examples of NON-vocabulary topics and their natural formats:
    • "Curious Questions" → child practices generating friendly, relevant questions after seeing a social picture
    • "Recognizing when someone needs help" → child looks at a scene and identifies what the person needs
    • "Saying goodbye" → child practices different goodbye phrases in different scenarios
    • "Taking turns in a game" → child identifies whose turn it is and what to say
- For vocabulary topics only: use a word-show-and-define format.
- Use "Nessa" as the character name (not "Bot"). Use "Child" for the child's turns.
- Past prompt library examples are style references only (short sentences, hints, praise, show_rewards). Ignore their content completely.

━━━ SCRIPT FORMAT ━━━
Show THREE ROUNDS for ONE example item from the lesson, demonstrating three response paths:
- Round 1: Child struggles → small hint → partial answer → bigger hint → correct
- Round 2: Child answers correctly on the first try
- Round 3: Child struggles → small hint → still wrong → bigger hint → still stuck → Nessa reveals answer → child repeats

Nessa rules: max 2 sentences per turn. Simple words. Praise before redirecting.
Hints: small = indirect/evocative. Big = explicit/structural. Each is a separate turn from the follow-up question.
After every success: show_rewards() (is_action: true), then a proud line, then transition.
